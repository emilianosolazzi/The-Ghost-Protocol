# The-Ghost-Protocol
GhostProtocol is a dark-fun, on-chain GameFi memecoin experiment where users submit evidence, earn GHOSTED rewards based on severity, and unlock story content through token-driven progression. Built with Solidity, React, and TypeScript, it blends verifiable on-chain actions with social gameplay and a bold cyber-noir interface.

"The Legend of the Great On-Chain Cold Shoulder 🥀⛓️
"In the beginning, there was the Whitepaper of Broken Promises. It was written in the tears of those who waited three business days for a 'Haha, sorry, just saw this!' text."
Humanity was tired of the Seen receipt. We needed something more immutable. Something that lived on Ethereum. So we forged the GHOSTED Protocol.

The Ghosting Receipt — Your On-Chain Proof of Pain
For 0.0095 ETH — about the price of a sad coffee — you mint a cryptographic proof of your humiliation.
You submit a hash of your evidence, a severity score from 1 to 100, and a drama type. The contract timestamps it forever. Nobody can edit it. Nobody can delete it. The blockchain remembers even when you wish it wouldn't.
30% of that fee goes straight to the Treasury. The remaining 70% stays in the protocol. And if you're the one who actually got ghosted — not submitting on someone else's behalf — the contract rewards you immediately in $GHOSTED tokens. Severity 100 pain pays 5,000 tokens. Severity 1 pays 50. The contract knows the difference between a slow fade and a full disappearing act.

The Proxy Witness
Not everyone submitting is the one who got ghosted. You can file on behalf of a friend — a proxy submission. The contract tracks this separately. Proxies get no token reward. They get only the quiet dignity of having done the right thing.
Direct victims: rewarded. Proxy witnesses: respected. The contract knows who bled.

The Locked Story
Every piece of evidence becomes a Locked Story. The details are hidden. If you want to read what really happened, you have to pay.
There are three ways in:
Burn your way in. Pay the unlock price in $GHOSTED. Half gets burned forever — gone from supply, gone from memory. Half goes to the person who submitted the story. You paid. They earned. The supply shrinks.
Credibility unlocks the door. If your credibility score is high enough — if you've held, staked, and proven yourself — you can read for free. And the act of reading boosts your score further. The trusted get more trust.
Pay in ETH. The submitter gets it all. No burn. A clean transaction between a reader and the person who lived it.
Every unlock makes the next one more expensive. Demand proves value. The story of real pain gets harder to access as more people want it.

The Ritual of Truth
Anyone can stake 100 $GHOSTED and declare: I believe this story is real — or — I think this is fake.
That stake is locked. The oracle — the trusted witness, the best friend who reviewed the screenshots — resolves it. Get it right: you receive 200 $GHOSTED back and your credibility score rises. Get it wrong: your stake is split. Half goes to the story's submitter. Half burns.
Win enough times in a row and your streak is recorded on-chain. Lose once and the streak resets to zero. The protocol remembers your track record.

The Credibility Score
This is the soul of the protocol. It is not bought. It is earned.
It rises when you assert truth correctly. It rises when you unlock stories through credibility. It rises slowly, through consistent honest behavior. A high score means the community trusts your submissions. Your evidence carries more weight. You are no longer just a person who got ghosted. You are a verified witness.
There is no shortcut. You cannot purchase credibility. You can only earn it by being right, repeatedly, over time.

The Burn
Every time a story is unlocked by burn, tokens are destroyed. Every time a truth assertion is wrong, tokens are destroyed. The supply only ever goes one direction.
The more the protocol is used — the more stories are unlocked, the more wrong guesses are made — the scarcer $GHOSTED becomes. Usage is the deflationary engine. Pain compounds into scarcity.

The Submitter's Revenue
If you submitted a story and locked it, you earn every time someone pays to read it. Token unlocks pay you half the price. ETH unlocks pay you everything. The more people want your story, the more you earn — and the more expensive it becomes to access.
Your humiliation is now a revenue-generating asset. The protocol did not forget. It monetized.

The Two-Step Power
Ownership of the protocol transfers in two steps. The current owner names a successor. The successor must accept. One signature is not enough. No accidental handoffs. No rug by typo.
The oracle can be replaced. The treasury address can be redirected. The whole protocol can be paused if something goes wrong. These are the safety valves — not glamorous, but they are what separate a real protocol from a jpeg with a wallet.

What the Contract Does Not Do
It does not read your messages. It does not know if she actually ghosted you. It does not verify the story is true — only that someone paid to submit it and someone else staked their tokens on whether it's real.
The truth lives in the screenshots. The contract holds only the hash. But the hash is permanent, timestamped, and chained to every unlock and assertion that follows it.

The blockchain never forgets. But it also never forgives.
